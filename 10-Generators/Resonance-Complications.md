# Resonance-Flavored Complications

Use these when a roll comes back 1–3 or a clock fills, and you want a complication that costs the crew something specific to *this* world rather than a generic Blades consequence.

## Resonance leak (item-driven)

- The seized item is humming audibly. A nearby civilian asks, politely, what that sound is.
- An Apex Aegis Anchor Scanner pings within 200ft. The team is two blocks away and pivoting.
- A Ministry shortwave intercept catches the Resonance signature. A bounty on the *crew* is being drafted right now.
- The item's owner is suddenly aware of where it is. They are walking toward it.
- A Twice-Born the crew has previously protected feels the leak and shows up to ask what they've done.

## Cover-up exposure

- A passerby has their phone out. Whatever just happened is on a 17-second video, already uploading.
- A child saw it. The child is telling their mother, who is telling the police.
- The cleanup crew that should have been there got rerouted by Apex Aegis. Whatever the crew leaves, stays.
- The Ministry liaison assigned to this district is the Mimic. The report will say whatever it wants to say.
- A rival firm's hunter watched the whole thing from a rooftop. Now they have leverage.

## Subway-adjacent

- A Subway entrance the crew was about to use has stopped letting them in. They don't know why.
- PTOLEMY's ledger has gone up by an entry the crew doesn't remember agreeing to.
- The Whisperer offers them information they didn't ask for, with a price they can't immediately understand.
- A merchant in the Subway has packed up overnight. The Decay Clock just ticked.
- The Crossroads Broker is in the back booth, nodding at one of them. Just one.

## Mimic-flavored

- A trusted contact remembers a thing they shouldn't — the wrong name, a habit that doesn't match this face.
- Two NPCs the crew trusts give the same warning, in the same words, on the same day, without having spoken.
- An NPC the crew assumed was dead sends them a coded message via dead drop.
- Something one of them said in private last session is being repeated back to them by a stranger.

## Omen-flavored *(use sparingly — these are seasoning, not entrees)*

- The streetlight above the meeting spot has been out for an hour. None of the others on the block are.
- A dog the crew has never seen before crosses their path twice in the same hour, going opposite directions.
- One PC's watch is running slow. They notice when it matters.
- Everyone in the diner stops talking at the same moment, then resumes mid-sentence.
- A child outside is drawing the same figure they drew on a different block last week.
