## Look — three details, no more

Pick weather + posture + a tell. Examples:

- Damp wool coat, stooped, never quite makes eye contact when she answers a direct question.
- Cigarette smoke in the hair, hands always slightly shaking, very fast smile that doesn't reach.
- Charcoal three-piece, perfect posture, taps his ring against the booth when he's about to lie.
- Off-the-rack grey suit, ink under the fingernails, sentences trail off when he's tired.
- Long oilskin, salt at the cuffs, talks to dogs more easily than to people.

## Mannerisms

- Apologizes for things that weren't her fault.
- Says "right, right" before disagreeing.
- Names every object by its full proper noun ("the Ostrov Dial" not "the dial").
- Repeats a question back before answering.
- Goes very still when surprised, like prey.
- Touches his pocket whenever an Anchor Item is mentioned in conversation.

## Drives

A short list of plain-language wants. The fewer words, the better.

- Wants out of this work.
- Wants a daughter back.
- Wants the Ministry to remember her name.
- Wants the bookstore on the corner to stay open one more year.
- Wants whoever did this to know it was him.
- Wants nothing — has wanted nothing for a long time.
