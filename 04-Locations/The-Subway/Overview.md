# The Subway — Overview

Underground neutral ground where non-evil monsters and vetted hunters coexist. Also the campaign's central unanswered question.

## What it is

Not metaphorically a subway — literally one. Or at least, the entrance is. A green-painted iron railing in a dead-end alley with a sign reading **Line 0 — Ethereal Express**. A businessman walks directly through where the railing stands. The crew sees it. They don't know why.

## How access works

The Subway's entrances are invisible to hostile or purely extractive presences. This is not a mechanical gate — it is a quality the Subway perceives.

- **Monsters chosen:** those without malicious intent toward the community.
- **Hunters chosen:** those whose reasons go beyond profit or institutional loyalty.
- **The crew:** access begins after Heist 2. Something about them — or that specific moment — qualified. They don't know what. *(See `04-Locations/The-Subway/Mysteries.md`.)*
- **Apex Aegis:** never found an entrance. Bribed hunters lose access immediately afterward.

There is **no Subway Token** mechanic. Access is who they are, not what they carry.

## What the Subway offers

- **The Fence** — buys stolen Biologicals and Relics for Coin. Half for Siphoned goods. Asks no questions. Remembers everything.
- **The Whisperer** — black market of information. Ministry personnel movements, Apex Aegis acquisition lists, which bounties are manufactured.
- **The Workshop** — Downtime activity to turn raw monster parts into permanent gear. Understands Anchor Items without destroying them.
- **The Job Board** — non-government jobs, anonymous postings, no Ministry or Apex monitoring.
- **The Crossroads Broker** — back booth deal-maker. See `03-NPCs/Active/Crossroads-Broker.md`.
- **PTOLEMY (The Appraiser)** — sentient luggage. Never lies. Always charges a favor. Keeps a ledger.

## Sensory details to reach for

- A monster in a business suit reading a newspaper.
- The smell of something that isn't quite food.
- A distant sound like wind through train tunnels that has no source.
- PTOLEMY's brass clasps clicking in patterns that translate, somehow.
- The Subway Decay Clock — once introduced — inked in red on a transit map mounted on the main hall wall.

## The Entrance Read-Aloud (Use when crew first finds it)

The entrance appears after the Emmerich score. They are not looking for it. They are in a dead-end alley, and it is just there.

> "The alley ends in a brick wall. That's what it should be. Except there's a green-painted iron railing set into the concrete at the far end — waist high, leading down, the kind of railing you'd find at any subway entrance in any city. A sign above it, tiled in white ceramic, reads: **LINE 0 — ETHEREAL EXPRESS**. The tiles look original. They look like they've been there since the twenties.
>
> A man in a suit rounds the corner behind you at full stride, phone to his ear, and walks directly through the railing without slowing. Through the sign. Through the top step. He doesn't stumble. He doesn't notice. He turns the corner at the end of the alley and is gone.
>
> The railing is still there. You can see it. You could reach out right now and put your hand on it and feel the cold paint. You didn't do anything to earn this. Nothing changed about you between the street and this alley. Something decided you qualify, and it didn't ask."

**GM note — the qualifying moment:** Something specific the crew did in the Emmerich score is what opened access. It is not about success or failure. It is about what they were willing to feel before they acted. The Subway perceived this. See `04-Locations/The-Subway/Mysteries.md` for the full range of qualifying behaviors — pick the one that fits what actually happened at your table.
