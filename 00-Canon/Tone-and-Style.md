# Tone & Style — Quick Reference

Industrial-occult noir. Quiet dread, not splatter horror. Moral ambiguity over melodrama. Tactile, sensory detail. Never cute. Never breezy about the cover-up.

## What it sounds like

The Subway smells of damp concrete and something underneath it that isn't quite food. PTOLEMY's brass clasps click in patterns that translate, somehow. Rain streaks the dusty windows of a bookstore that takes in twenty dollars on purpose. A Ministry courier drops a damp manila envelope on the counter and walks out without speaking.

Reach for: weather, smell, texture, the hum of bad fluorescent light, small mundane details that survive into supernatural moments. The body of a hunted Twice-Born wears a wedding ring. The Barghest's locker has a keychain photo of children long dead.

## What it does not sound like

No quips at the wrong moment. No splatter for shock. No description of the supernatural that resolves into "cool." If it lands as cool, the human cost has not been surfaced yet.

Avoid: the word "epic," exclamation marks in narration, monsters that are *only* threats, NPCs whose tragedy is loud rather than buried.

## The cover-up

Always sacred. Exposure is not a setback; it is the end of a career and a license and possibly a life. When a complication needs weight, reach for exposure risk before reaching for damage. A witness with a phone is scarier than a wound.

## Moral ambiguity

The Ministry believes it is doing the right thing — that is what makes it dangerous. Apex Aegis is not just greedy; it is existentially reckless. Twice-Born are not all sympathetic. Hunters are not all clean. The crew is not heroic. The campaign earns its weight by refusing to flatten any of these.

## Anchor Items

Treat every seizure as a transaction with a person, not a creature. The Anchor Ledger names the victim. The Three-Layer Rule applies even to one-scene monsters. If a player goes for the Anchor without acknowledging the cost, surface it in the fiction — the photograph in the locker, the worn leather of the dog collar, the way the locket has been opened and closed enough times to wear the hinge thin.

## The Subway

Wondrous and uneasy, never whimsical. It chooses, it doesn't grant. PTOLEMY's ledger is always accurate and always comes due. The Crossroads Broker offers exactly what the crew wants and takes something they will miss later — the GM rolls in secret.

## Pacing rule of thumb

Quiet dread accumulates in scenes the players don't realize are doing work. Do not state the threat. Let the Omen Clock symptoms land in the periphery — clocks running slow, dogs refusing to cross a street, two unrelated people describing the same dream.
