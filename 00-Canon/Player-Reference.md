# Player Reference — *The Shadow Exchange*

Print one per player. Keep it next to your character sheet.

---

## What's different from D&D

You play a small monster-hunting firm operating under a government cover-up. The world believes monsters aren't real. They are. You hunt them, catalogue them, sometimes sell their parts on the black market — and you keep the public believing the cover story.

| In D&D / Pathfinder | In Blades |
|---|---|
| Attack roll + damage roll | One roll. Most rolls produce both an outcome *and* a complication. |
| HP attrition | **Stress** (rerolls / pushes / resistance) and **Harm** (slow). |
| Class features and spell slots | **Action ratings** (broad categories) and **special abilities** (playbook). |
| Plan before acting | **Engagement roll** drops you into the score. Plan retroactively via flashback. |
| GM rolls vs you | GM never rolls. All rolls are yours. |
| Loot is loot | Some loot has a name. The Anchor Item of a disguised monster *is* a person's tether to the human world. |

---

## The action loop

**Engagement roll → into the score → action rolls → consequences → resistance (optional) → score ends → downtime → next score.**

You will be in the middle of the heist before you've finished planning. That's the design. Anything you needed to prep — *you can flash back to having done it.*

---

## Action rolls

Roll a number of **d6** equal to your action rating.

| Highest die | Result |
|---|---|
| 6 | **Full success.** What you wanted. |
| 4–5 | **Mixed success.** You do it, but with a cost. |
| 1–3 | **Bad outcome.** Consequences. |
| Two 6s | **Critical.** Bonus on top. |

Position (Controlled / Risky / Desperate) determines how bad the consequences are. Effect (Limited / Standard / Great) determines how much you accomplish.

**Always know your Position and Effect before you roll.** Ask the GM if they don't say.

---

## Stress

Stress is your reroll currency. Spend it to:

- **Push yourself.** +1d to a roll, or improve effect by one level. Cost: 2 stress.
- **Assist** another PC. +1d to their roll. Cost: 1 stress.
- **Resist a consequence.** Roll Insight / Prowess / Resolve, spend (6 minus highest die) stress, reduce or negate the consequence.
- **Flash back.** Spend 0 / 1 / 2 stress to retroactively have done a thing.

Max stress: **9.** When you hit 9, you take a **Trauma** and reset to 0.

---

## Trauma

Trauma is *good* mechanically. You earn XP for playing it in a way that hurts your character's interests. Don't avoid it — play into it.

When you take Trauma, mark a trauma type (Cold / Haunted / Obsessed / Paranoid / Reckless / Soft / Unstable / Vicious) and tell the GM which one. It becomes part of how your character is in the world.

After 4 traumas, your character retires.

---

## Resistance — use it

When a consequence lands, **ask for a resistance roll** if it matters to you. The GM should prompt you in early sessions. Don't accept Trauma you could have resisted three rolls ago.

Resistance attribute matches the consequence:

- Physical harm or rushed action → **Prowess**
- Mental / emotional / supernatural → **Resolve**
- Surprise / observation / setup → **Insight**

---

## Flashbacks — how to use them

You realize mid-heist that you'd want a tool, an alibi, a contact, a key, a backup plan. You can have *already done* the prep:

- *"I flash back to acquiring an Asset for this — a forged Ministry badge."*
- *"I flash back to talking to my Friend NPC about the building's layout."*
- *"I flash back to having modified my Anchor-detection scanner before we left."*

Cost in stress depends on how plausible the flashback is. The GM tells you.

---

## Downtime — what you do between scores

Each PC takes:

1. **Indulge Vice.** Clear stress equal to your highest die. Overindulge = trouble.
2. **One downtime activity** (Acquire Asset / Long-Term Project / Reduce Heat / Train / Recover).
3. **Pay coin or take Heat** for lifestyle.

Plus: roll for **Entanglements** if Heat is high enough.

This is where character growth happens. *Don't skip downtime.*

---

## What the GM will keep saying

- *"What's your Position and Effect?"*
- *"You can resist that — want to?"*
- *"Want to flash back to something?"*

These are not trick questions. The honest answer to each is a real choice that shapes what comes next.

---

## Shadow Exchange — what to remember about *this* world

- **The cover-up is sacred.** Public exposure of the supernatural ends careers and licenses. Witnesses with phones are scarier than wounds.
- **Some monsters are people.** Anchor Items are personal — a thimble from a daughter, a leather collar from a first dog. Taking them has weight. The crew keeps a ledger.
- **The Subway is real and you don't fully understand it.** It chose you. You don't know why.
- **Apex Aegis is the corporate threat.** Recklessly profitable, eroding the cover-up.
- **The Mimic is the internal threat.** Already wearing a face you trust.

---

## Trusted resources

- **PTOLEMY** — Subway appraiser. Never lies. Always charges a favor.
- **Harlan Okafor** — Diner outside the city. Ex-Ministry. Knows things, charges fair prices.
- **The Whisperer** — Subway. Trades in Ministry and Apex Aegis intel.
- **The Fence** — Subway. No questions. Half price for Siphoned goods.
- **The Crossroads Broker** — Subway back booth. Offers what you want. Takes what you'll miss.

---

*One last thing: this campaign is industrial-occult noir. Quiet dread, moral ambiguity, weather and texture. The work is interesting because it costs something. Take the costs. Spend the stress. Earn the trauma.*
