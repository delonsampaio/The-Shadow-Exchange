# GM Cheat Sheet

Print and have at the table. Designed for a GM running their first Blades campaign.

---

## The action loop

**Engagement → Action Roll → Consequence → Resistance (optional) → Repeat → Score ends → Downtime**

Don't let players plan too long. After 10 minutes of bookshop talk, **call the engagement roll** and start the score *in medias res*.

---

## Position & Effect — say them out loud

| Position | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Controlled** | They have leverage. Worst case: a setback they can recover from. |
| **Risky** | Default. Real danger, viable plan. |
| **Desperate** | They are out of leverage. Failure is harm, costs, or escalation. |

| Effect | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Limited** | Partial result. They make progress but not enough. |
| **Standard** | Normal result. |
| **Great** | Full result, plus something extra (intel, an opening, a leg up). |

**Negotiate trade-offs in fiction:** "You can do this Risky/Standard, or trade it down to Controlled/Limited if you take an extra minute." Players love this.

---

## Resistance — push for it

When a consequence lands and a player doesn't like it, prompt:

> *"You can resist that. Roll [Insight / Prowess / Resolve], spend 6 minus your highest die in stress. Do you want to?"*

The first three sessions, **prompt this every time.** They will not ask on their own.

---

## Stress and Trauma

- Push yourself or assist or resist: **+2 stress** typically.
- Hit 9 stress: **take a Trauma**, reset to zero. This is good — Trauma earns XP and shapes character.
- Trauma triggers: tell the player when their Trauma is fictionally relevant. They earn XP for playing it.

---

## Flashbacks — actively invite them

Flashbacks let players retroactively prep. Cost is **1 stress** (Risky), **2 stress** (Desperate), **0 stress** (Controlled / something they obviously could have done).

Lines to use at the table:

- *"Want to flash back to having grabbed that during downtime?"*
- *"Did anyone do anything clever between sessions that solves this?"*
- *"Take 1 stress and tell me what you set up earlier this week."*

Use these twice in the first hour of Session 1.

---

## Consequences — the menu

When a roll fails or comes up mixed, pick from:

- **Reduced effect** — they get some, not all.
- **Complication** — a problem appears (clock fills, NPC arrives, position worsens for next roll).
- **Lost opportunity** — door closes. Try the *other* approach next.
- **Worse position** — Risky → Desperate.
- **Harm** — physical or mental damage.

Default to *complication* on a mixed result. Default to *complication + worse position* on a 1–3.

---

## When to tick the Detection Clock

- Player makes noise (literal or fictional).
- Loud action: a bullet fired, a door kicked.
- A failed Prowl, a failed Sway with a guard.
- A consequence the GM picks instead of harm.
- A passing Apex Aegis Anchor Scanner ping while carrying loot.

Tick it **visibly.** Players who can see the clock can make better decisions.

---

## Downtime activities — the four

For each PC after a score:

1. **Indulge Vice** — clear stress equal to highest die. Overindulge = trouble.
2. **Acquire Asset** — roll Tier of crew + bonuses, get a temporary item or service.
3. **Long-Term Project** — work on a clock toward a personal goal (research, modification, relationship).
4. **Reduce Heat** — roll, lower crew Heat by 1+.
5. **Train** — gain XP in an attribute area.

Plus: **Recover** (Physick roll, advance harm-clearing clock) and **Pay Coin or take Heat** for lifestyle.

## Shadow Exchange — campaign-specific reminders

### The Three-Layer Rule
Every monster has: **(1) what it appears to be, (2) what it actually is, (3) why it became that.** Generate all three before play. The Tragic Angle is what makes the moral weight land.

### Anchor Items — moral weight
Every disguised monster has one. Seizing it = 1d6 hours until disguise fails. **Log every seizure to the Anchor Ledger with the victim's name.** The campaign's conscience lives in that file.

### Cover-up exposure — frame it as the complication
Witnesses, phones, leaked documents are scarier than damage. When you need a bad consequence and don't want to do harm, reach for **exposure risk** before reaching for a wound.

### Detection Clock — every heist
4-seg. Resets after the score. Full = Ministry or Apex Aegis arrives.

### Spoilage Clock — Sessions 3–4 onward
3-seg per Biological. Ticks each Downtime. Paused by Cold Storage. **Don't introduce until their first Biological starts to matter.**

### Tragedy Clock — Monkey's Paw only
4-seg. **GM ticks secretly.** Players know something is coming.

### Subway Decay Clock — Session 5+
6-seg. Visible on the Subway transit map. Ticks from Mimic activity, exposure events, Resonance leaks.

### Omen Clock — GM-secret, mid-game
8-seg. Players see symptoms: stray animals, clocks running slow, identical dreams. Don't tick before the clock is officially live.

### Custom mechanics
- **Double Bookkeeping** — Sway after a Ministry bounty to skim one item.
- **The Appraiser's Gamble** — Study or Consort with PTOLEMY for item identification.
- **Twice-Born Favor** — earned by protecting Twice-Born; max 3 held; spend on Safe Harbor / Rare Items / Lore.
- **Crossroads Cost** — d6 rolled secretly *(Memory / Relationship / Year / Future Favor / Truth / Name)*.

## When you're not sure what to do

1. Ask "what is the most interesting consequence?"
2. Ask "what would the cover-up cost here?"
3. Ask "whose name is on the Anchor I'm about to take?"
4. If still stuck: ask the table.

---

## The four lines you'll say most

- *"What's your Position and Effect?"*
- *"You can resist that — want to?"*
- *"Want to flash back to something?"*
- *"What does this cost the cover-up?"*
