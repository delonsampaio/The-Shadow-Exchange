# The Shadow Exchange — Dictionary

Strict definitions of homebrew concepts. These mechanics must never be conflated with generic fantasy tropes.

* **Anchor:** An everyday object tying a supernatural entity to the physical world. It always has a human cost or memory attached to it. Seizing it is a transaction with a person, not just taking loot.
* **Biological:** A physical, harvestable piece of a supernatural entity. Highly valuable but rots quickly. Must be stored in a Cold Storage Locker or it triggers a Spoilage Clock.
* **Crossroads Broker:** An entity that offers exactly what the crew wants but takes something they will miss later. The cost is tracked in the Crossroads Cost Ledger.
* **Double Bookkeeping:** A specialized crew ability to successfully fudge Ministry paperwork, allowing the crew to skim an Anchor, Relic, or Biological off a government bounty without detection.
* **The Ministry:** The bureaucratic, government arm that issues bounties on the supernatural. Dangerous because they believe they are doing the moral, correct thing. Cover-ups are sacred to them.
* **PTOLEMY:** The brass-clasped ledger or entity within the Subway that records transactions. It is perfectly accurate, completely amoral, and always comes due.
* **Relic:** A supernatural item that does not spoil, but leaks "Resonance" if not stored in a Lead-Lined Vault.
* **Resonance:** The sensory, tactile leakage of the supernatural into the mundane world (e.g., cold spots, clocks running slow, static, the smell of ozone). Not a magical aura.
* **The Subway:** The wondrous, uneasy, underground network/dimension utilized by the Twice-Born. It chooses; it does not grant. It is never whimsical.
* **Twice-Born:** Individuals who have returned from the Subway or changed states. They are not mindless monsters; they wear wedding rings, carry photos of dead children, and exist in a state of quiet tragedy.

## AI Lore Directives
* **Trope Avoidance:** Do not use the words "magic", "spell", "mana", or "epic". Supernatural effects must be described via their tactile, sensory impact on the environment (Resonance) rather than flashy visual effects.
* **The Cover-Up:** Exposure of these elements to the mundane public is not a mere setback; it is an existential threat to the crew.
