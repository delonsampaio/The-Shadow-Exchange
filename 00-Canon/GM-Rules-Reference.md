# GM Rules Reference — *The Shadow Exchange*

Four things the rest of the campaign files don't fully cover. Read this once before Session 0. The GM Cheat Sheet handles fast recall at the table; this document handles the "wait, what does that mean" moments.

---

## Part 1 — The 12 Actions

You name the action before the player rolls. They describe what their character is doing; you say "that's Hunt" or "that's Consort." The rule is: players pick what their character *does*, you pick the action it maps to.

Many actions overlap. That's intentional. If someone describes something that could go two ways, hear them out — the fiction decides.

---

### INSIGHT — the thinking attribute

**Hunt** — carefully track a target.
Follow someone's movements. Discover where someone or something is. Set up an ambush position. Attack with precise ranged fire. Read the room for threats before entering.
*In this campaign: identifying a Twice-Born by their behavioral patterns. Tracking Apex Aegis surveilance rotations. Watching a target long enough to time an extraction.*

**Study** — scrutinize details and interpret evidence.
Research documents, files, books. Recall specialized knowledge about a monster or entity. Analyze a person for deception or true feeling. Read a scene after the fact to understand what happened.
*In this campaign: identifying an entity type from physical evidence. Reading Ministry paperwork for anomalies. Cross-referencing the Hunter's Journal with a new target.*

**Survey** — observe the situation and anticipate outcomes.
Spot trouble before it arrives. Case a location before going in. Read the crowd for exits, threats, witnesses. Detect someone's intentions from how they carry themselves.
*In this campaign: reading a Twice-Born for how close they are to an episode. Anticipating when a Ministry scanner van will pass. Spotting the Apex Aegis tail before it matters.*

> **Hunt vs. Survey:** Hunt is focused on a *specific target*. Survey is reading an *environment or situation*.

**Tinker** — fiddle with devices and mechanisms.
Pick a lock or crack a safe. Build, modify, or repair a piece of gear. Disable an alarm, trap, or surveillance system. Operate technical equipment with precision. Perform careful physical work on a body or specimen.
*In this campaign: operating the Scanner Station to isolate a frequency. Extracting a biological sample cleanly. Modifying a containment case for a specific entity type.*

---

### PROWESS — the body attribute

**Finesse** — employ dextrous manipulation or subtle misdirection.
Pickpocket. Handle a vehicle or mount with precision. Fight with technique in a formal or controlled way. Pass something off undetected. Do something delicate under pressure.
*In this campaign: palming an Anchor Item in front of its owner. Maneuvering the firm's van through a narrow dock access. Slipping a forged document into a Ministry courier's bag.*

**Prowl** — traverse skillfully and quietly.
Sneak past a guard or through a crowd. Move through a building without leaving a trace. Attack from concealment. Cross difficult terrain without being noticed.
*In this campaign: entering a target's home without triggering an Apex scanner. Tailing a subject through Old City without losing them in the crowd. Getting into position before the entity realizes you're there.*

**Skirmish** — entangle a target in close combat so they can't easily escape.
Brawl. Wrestle. Fight with weapons in close quarters. Hold a position under pressure. Prevent someone from getting away.
*In this campaign: physically subduing a Twice-Born mid-episode without killing them. Holding a door against something trying to get through it. Fighting off an Apex Aegis extraction team at close range.*

> **Skirmish vs. Finesse:** Skirmish is messy, committed, close. Finesse is precise, controlled, technical.

**Wreck** — unleash savage force.
Smash through an obstacle. Destroy something deliberately. Use brute force to make a point or create chaos. Breach a door, wall, or barrier.
*In this campaign: forcing a cold storage unit that's been welded shut. Destroying surveillance equipment on your way out. Creating enough noise and chaos that Apex pulls back.*

---

### RESOLVE — the will attribute

**Attune** — open your mind to arcane power.
Perceive Resonance. Communicate with or perceive supernatural entities. Sense the nature or origin of an occult object or event. Extend awareness past normal senses into what the Ministry calls "Ethereal Bleed."
*In this campaign: reading the Resonance signature of an Anchor Item to identify its owner. Detecting whether a Twice-Born is present in a space. Sensing when the Omen Clock is manifesting a new symptom.*

**Command** — compel swift obedience.
Intimidate. Issue orders that get followed. Lead a group under pressure. Establish authority through presence rather than argument.
*In this campaign: getting a Twice-Born to stop mid-episode without a fight. Holding the crew together when something goes wrong mid-score. Making a Ministry courier drop an official posture.*

**Consort** — socialize with friends and contacts.
Build rapport. Make a good impression. Access resources, people, or places through relationships. Connect through shared background or heritage. Network.
*In this campaign: getting information out of Harlan at the diner. Building standing with a Subway vendor. Reaching a Twice-Born through their mundane social life before they know why you're there.*

**Sway** — influence with guile, charm, or argument.
Lie convincingly. Persuade. Manipulate. Argue someone into or out of a position. Use social pressure rather than relationship.
*In this campaign: talking past a Ministry inspector at the door. Convincing a witness they saw a gas leak. Negotiating a better price at the Fence without your position changing.*

> **Consort vs. Sway:** Consort works through relationship — they like or trust you. Sway works through argument or pressure — whether they like you or not.
>
> **Command vs. Sway:** Command uses authority or fear. Sway uses persuasion or manipulation. You can Command someone who's afraid of you; you Sway someone you need to convince.

## Part 2 — Harm

When a consequence is physical or mental damage, you assign harm by level. Harm is *slow* — it accumulates rather than resolving immediately.

| Level | Name | Mechanical Effect | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Lesser | Reduced Effect on relevant rolls | Battered, Drained, Distracted, Scared, Confused |
| **2** | Moderate | −1d on relevant rolls | Exhausted, Deep Cut, Concussion, Panicked |
| **3** | Severe | *Need Help* — can't act without another PC assisting | Broken Leg, Shot, Badly Burned, Terrified |
| **4** | Fatal | Out of action. Death or equivalent catastrophe | Electrocuted, Stabbed in the Heart |

**Two Level 1 harms** accumulate into a Level 2 if a PC takes a second Level 1 in the same category. Two Level 2s accumulate into a Level 3.

**Resistance can reduce harm by one level** before it's recorded. A Level 3 consequence resisted becomes Level 2. A Level 2 resisted becomes Level 1.

**Healing:** PCs recover harm through the *Recover* downtime activity — roll Physick (a Tinker-adjacent action). Each roll advances a healing clock. Level 1 clears after one clock fill. Level 2 and 3 take longer and may require proper medical equipment.

**In this campaign, reach for harm last.** The cover-up is more threatening than a wound. A witness with a phone, a Detection Clock tick, a complication that puts the crew in a worse position — these are your default consequences. Use harm when the fiction demands it: something is actively trying to hurt someone.

## Part 3 — Crew XP and Advancement

At the **end of every session**, review the four XP triggers. Mark 1 XP for each that occurred. Mark 2 XP if something happened in a major way or multiple times.

### The four crew XP triggers

**1. Campaign-specific trigger (Hunters — Shadow Exchange):**
*"Complete a score that protects the cover-up, or take meaningful action to protect a Twice-Born from extraction or exposure."*
Mark XP if the crew finished a job and the public cover story held. Also mark XP if they made a significant choice to protect a Twice-Born — warned them, shielded them, returned their Anchor, helped them disappear.

**2. Contend with challenges above your current station.**
Mark XP if the crew tangled with a higher-Tier faction, a more dangerous opponent than expected, or a situation that genuinely outclassed them and they dealt with it anyway.

**3. Bolster your crew's reputation or develop a new one.**
Mark XP if something the crew did this session changed how they're known — in the Subway, among other firms, with Ministry contacts, or in their neighborhood.

**4. Express the goals, drives, inner conflict, or essential nature of the crew.**
Mark XP if something happened that highlighted what makes *this* crew specific. The moral weight of the Anchor system. The tension between government work and the black market. A moment that felt like *The Shadow Exchange* specifically.

### When the track fills (8 XP)

Clear the track and take **one** of:
- A new crew **special ability** (see Part 4 below)
- **Two crew upgrade boxes** (training, lair improvements, quality, etc.)

Say in one sentence how the crew obtained it. Where did it come from? What changed?

## Part 4 — Hunters Crew Special Abilities

The crew starts with **Double Bookkeeping** (already on the Crew Sheet). Session 0 adds **one more** from this list.

These are the abilities available to a Hunters-type crew in *The Shadow Exchange*. When the crew earns an advancement later, they can pick from this list again.

---

**Deadly**
Each PC may add +1 action rating to Hunt, Prowl, or Skirmish (max rating 3).
Each player chooses their own action — you don't all have to pick the same one. If taken at creation, it supersedes the normal starting limit.
*Good for a crew that leans into field efficiency.*

---

**Anointed**
+1d to resistance rolls against supernatural threats. +1d to recovery rolls when you have supernatural harm.
*Essential if the crew expects to take hits from the things they hunt. First pick for any table that worries about survival.*

---

**Forged in the Fire**
Each PC gets +1d to resistance rolls.
*Broader than Anointed — applies to all consequences, not just supernatural ones. Good if the crew will be running high-heat scores.*

---

**Death Veil**
You don't take extra Heat when killing is involved on a score.
Standard jobs generate Heat based on how much noise you made. This ability strips out the penalty for fatalities — the cleanup is that good.
*Best for a crew that expects Twice-Born episodes to turn lethal.*

---

**No Traces**
When you keep an operation quiet or make it look like an accident, you get half the Rep value of the target (round up) instead of zero. When you end downtime with zero Heat, take +1 Rep.
*Rewards the crew for playing the cover-up straight. Strong for a low-heat, high-reputation strategy.*

---

**Synchronized**
When you perform a group action, multiple 6s from different rolls count as a critical success.
The leader takes Stress for each teammate who rolled 1–3, as normal — but the upside scales with the whole crew.
*Best for a crew that functions as a team rather than specialists working separately.*

---

**Pack Rats**
When you roll to acquire an asset, take +1d.
Represents the lair's accumulated gear and the crew's black-market contacts.
*Good if the crew plans to use the Acquire Asset downtime activity frequently — unusual gear for unusual targets.*

---

**Accord**
You may treat up to three faction statuses at +3 as if they were turf, for Tier advancement purposes.
*A mid-game pick. Not useful at Tier 0, but significant once the crew has built relationships with the Subway, the Twice-Born, or reform-minded Ministry contacts.*

---

### Quick pick guide for Session 0

| If the table wants... | Take... |
|---|---|
| Tougher characters, harder to put down | **Anointed** or **Forged in the Fire** |
| Better at hunting and fighting | **Deadly** |
| Lower heat, better reputation | **No Traces** or **Death Veil** |
| Stronger teamwork | **Synchronized** |
| Better gear and resource access | **Pack Rats** |

**Recommendation for a first session:** Anointed or Forged in the Fire. New players will take more consequences than experienced ones, and resistance rolls are the system's pressure release valve. Making resistance cheaper early keeps the table from feeling overwhelmed.

## A note on the SRD

The Blades SRD (in `00-Canon/Blades-SRD.html`) does not include specific crew playbooks — the Hunters type is custom to this campaign. The special abilities above are drawn from the SRD's generic ability list and filtered for a monster-hunting crew. If a player invents an ability concept that isn't on this list, you can work it out with them — the system is designed for that.
