# The Hunter Underground

**One-line read** — independent hunters with no institutional loyalty. Distrust everyone. Know more than they share. Gather at Harlan's.

## Agenda

Survive the work. Protect their own. Keep the Ministry and Apex Aegis at arm's length. Remember the dead.

## Methods

- **Dead Drops** — coded messages at specific locations: a symbol carved into a diner booth, a classified ad in the back of a niche magazine. The crew can use these to receive tips or leave warnings.
- **Reputation** — word moves fast about who can be trusted and who gets civilians killed.
- **The Hunter's Journal** — handwritten field manual passed between hunters. The crew has a copy in the Containment Suite.

## Key NPCs

- **Harlan Okafor** — see `03-NPCs/Active/Harlan-Okafor.md`.
- **The Wall of the Missing** — corkboard at Harlan's. Photos, names, case numbers of hunters who didn't come back. Anyone can add. Anyone can take a case from it. The crew's names are not on it yet.
- **The AM Host** — late-night radio host who believes in everything. Frequently describes real events badly. Never wrong about a location. Always wrong about the cause.
- **The Rival Hunter** *(Job 8 — Away Job)* — independent, doesn't like the crew's reputation, isn't entirely wrong about some of it.

## Intel Advantage

Jobs heard at Harlan's are 24–48 hours ahead of Ministry lists. This advantage disappears the moment Apex Aegis locates the diner.

## How to play them

Loyal in private, brittle in public. Half of them owe Harlan a tab. Most have lost someone the Wall now remembers. They will help if you've helped — and never if you haven't.
