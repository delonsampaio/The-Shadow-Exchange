# The Ministry

**One-line read** — the government body that issues bounties, licenses hunters, and maintains the cover-up at all costs. Believes it is doing the right thing — which makes it more dangerous than Apex Aegis in some ways.

## Agenda

Prevent the public from knowing monsters exist. Maintain the cover-up across generations. Treat all monsters as threats. Founding doctrine holds that humanity cannot coexist with knowledge of the supernatural — that exposure would cause civilizational breakdown. Whether this is true is a question the campaign can explore.

## Methods

- **Bounty system** — Public bounty lists for licensed firms, with Resonance scanners flagging targets.
- **Mandatory Anchor Item destruction** — Tier 1 Contraband classification, automatic destruction order on seizure.
- **License revocation** — Any hunter or firm that allows public exposure loses their license instantly and faces criminal charges. The industry's most severe sanction.
- **Cleanup teams** — Field cleanup is sometimes adequate, sometimes worryingly thin. Apex Aegis has compromised parts of the chain.
- **Resonance scanners** — Standard issue, mile-radius detection of Anchor Item signatures.

## Key NPCs

- **The Ministry liaison** assigned to the crew's district *(name TBD — possible Mimic candidate per bible guidance)*.
- **Ministry couriers** — uniformed, do not speak, deliver damp manila envelopes.

## History with the crew

- The crew is licensed and active. They pay their dues by completing bounties. The relationship is functional and cold.

## What would shift their tier or hold

- A major exposure event the crew either causes or fails to contain.
- Internal exposure of the Apex Aegis bounty-manipulation pipeline (this would partially repair Ministry credibility).
- A senior Ministry figure publicly compromised.

## Notes

The Ministry is not the campaign villain. It is a structural force. The crew's discomfort with it should accumulate through small specific moments — the wording of an incident report, the indifference of a courier, the destruction order on an Anchor Item that belonged to someone they liked.
