# The Subway Community

**One-line read** — non-evil monsters and vetted hunters coexisting on neutral ground beneath the city. Neither welcoming nor hostile. Watching.

## Agenda

Survive. Remain hidden. Trade fairly. Keep the Mimic from rotting it from inside and Apex Aegis from finding it from outside.

## Methods

The Subway acts through individual operators rather than centralized policy. Reputation is the currency — who can be trusted, who gets civilians killed, who returns favors.

## Key NPCs

- **PTOLEMY** *(The Appraiser)* — sentient luggage. Never lies. Always charges a favor. Keeps a ledger. See `03-NPCs/Active/PTOLEMY.md`.
- **The Fence** — buys stolen Biologicals and Relics for Coin. Pays half for Siphoned goods. Asks no questions. Remembers everything.
- **The Whisperer** — black market of information. Ministry personnel movements, Apex Aegis acquisition lists, which government bounties are manufactured vs. genuine.
- **The Workshop** — turns raw monster parts into permanent gear. One of the few places that understands how to work with Anchor Items without destroying them.
- **The Job Board** — non-government heist jobs, anonymous postings, no Ministry oversight, no Apex Aegis monitoring.
- **The Crossroads Broker** — back booth, 1987 suit. See `03-NPCs/Active/Crossroads-Broker.md`.

## The Selection — what gets in

Not a mechanical gate. The Subway perceives a quality.

- **Monsters chosen:** those without malicious intent toward the community. Not necessarily good — just not predatory toward this space and its people.
- **Hunters chosen:** those whose reasons go beyond profit or institutional loyalty. The Subway appears to detect purpose, conscience, grief, genuine care for something beyond themselves.
- **Apex Aegis cannot find it.** Despite vast resources and years of effort. Bribed hunters lose access immediately afterward.

## Decay Clock — what advances it

- Mimic activity.
- Mundane exposure events.
- Resonance leaks the crew fails to contain.
- *(At 4 segments: merchants begin packing up and some Twice-Born flee. At 6: cover-up collapses. Campaign endgame begins.)*

## How to play it

Wondrous and uneasy, never whimsical. Two or three sensory details per visit, not a tour. Let the players ask questions; answer some, not all.
