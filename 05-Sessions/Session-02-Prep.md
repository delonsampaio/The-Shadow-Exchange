## Opening scene — research and pursuit

> **READ ALOUD — the shopping district:**
>
> "Mid-morning on a weekday. The shopping district is loud in a way that makes the Resonance scanner readouts hard to parse. Buses. Strollers. A construction crew tearing up the corner where they always tear up the corner. Emmerich's shop is on the second floor of a narrow building on the quiet side of the block — a window painted with the words **EMMERICH & DAUGHTER, BESPOKE TAILOR** in gold leaf. The 'and Daughter' has been there a long time."

If they observe before acting, give them this:

> **READ ALOUD — the observation window:**
>
> "Through the upstairs window, you can see him. Slight man, neat moustache, measuring tape around his neck. He's measuring a small child for a school uniform — turning the child gently by the shoulders, marking the chalk on the sleeve, asking a question that makes the child laugh. There is a woman waiting on the bench — the child's mother, maybe — and Emmerich asks after her own mother by name. They have known each other for a long time. Probably their whole lives. Probably more than that.
>
> He is a tailor. He has always been a tailor."

## The Choice — three options, not two

**A. Seize the thimble.** Ministry payout. Emmerich loses his disguise in 1d6 hours. He has nowhere to go.
- *Reward:* significant Coin. Twice-Born network now knows the crew as predators.
- *Loss:* no Twice-Born Favor. **A Twice-Born Favor opportunity will be harder to earn going forward.**

**B. Warn him and walk away.** No payout. He goes into hiding. Bounty stays active.
- *Reward:* **+1 Twice-Born Favor.** The network knows the crew as protectors.
- *Loss:* no Coin. Possible Heat with the Ministry.

**C. Partial complicity — take the thimble, but warn him first.** Smaller Ministry payout. Emmerich gets the 1d6-hour window to disappear. He remembers it as **ambivalence**, not betrayal.
- *Reward:* half Coin, no Twice-Born Favor *(but no enmity earned either)*.
- *Loss:* the Twice-Born network learns the crew is *transactional*. 

### Mid-scene read-alouds

> **The thimble, if it's seized:**
>
> "It's small enough to disappear in a closed fist. Worn smooth on the inside from 200 years of one specific finger. There's an inscription you'd miss at a glance — a name in a language you don't read, in handwriting that wasn't his. The metal is warm. Warmer than it should be."

> **Emmerich, if warned:**
>
> "He doesn't move at first. He looks at the child's mother through the front window — she is laughing at something on her phone — and then back at you. He says, very quietly: 'How long do I have?' He does not look surprised. He has been waiting for this conversation for a long time."

> **The Apex Aegis Peacekeeper team, if they ping:**
>
> "Two of them, dark coats, earpieces. But they aren't carrying weapons drawn; they are carrying clipboards. The closer one stops in the middle of the crosswalk, checking a Resonance scanner disguised as a tablet. 'Unlicensed extraction detected,' he says into his comms, his voice perfectly, terrifyingly bored. 'Flagging Curios & Chronicles for a compliance audit. Let Director Vance know.' He puts a finger to his ear and starts walking, fast, in your direction."

## The Twist — first sight of the Subway

### GM REMINDER: Prompting a Flashback During the Chase
As they run from the drones into the dead-end alley, before they notice the Subway entrance, wait for a player to panic. Then, pause the game and offer this:
> *"You are trapped in a dead end. But you didn't walk into this blind. We are pausing the clock. Does anyone want to spend 1 Stress to Flash Back to yesterday, to tell me what you bribed, hacked, or stashed in this alley to help you escape right now?"*
*(If they do, let them! But the Subway entrance reveals itself anyway).*

> **READ ALOUD — the alley:**
>
> "You round the corner into a dead-end alley, Aegis drones buzzing behind you. But right there — built into the concrete — is a green-painted iron railing leading down. A sign above it reads: **Line 0 — Ethereal Express**.
>
> You look back toward the street. A businessman in a suit walks directly through where the railing stands. Like it isn't there. Like it doesn't exist.
>
> But you can see it. And you don't know why. You didn't do anything. Nothing changed. Something decided you qualify, and nobody asked your permission."

### The descent — guided first visit

> **READ ALOUD — the platform:**
>
> "The stairs go further down than they should. The Aegis drones do not follow. At the bottom, a vaulted hall lit in greenish light. The architecture is wrong — partly Beaux-Arts, partly subway, partly something else. There are people here. Some of them aren't.
>
> A monster in a three-piece suit is reading yesterday's newspaper on a bench. He glances at you, registers what you are, returns to his paper. The smell isn't quite food — woodsmoke, maybe, and something metallic. From somewhere down a tunnel that does not exist on any map you've ever seen, a sound like wind through train tunnels, but there is no wind.
>
> A wall-mounted transit map of the city is mounted above a long bench, inked in clean black lines. 
>
> In the corner: a piece of luggage. Brass clasps, cracked leather, no visible eyes. The clasps click in patterns. You realize, after a second, that the clicking has a rhythm. After another second, you realize the rhythm has stopped. It is listening to you."

Then, walk one of the PCs past three stations: the **Job Board**, the **Fence's stall**, and the **Crossroads Broker's back booth**.

> **READ ALOUD — the back booth:**
>
> "There's a booth at the very back of the hall — too far back, somehow, given how big the hall actually is. One man at the booth, alone. Gray suit, narrow tie, an attaché case he is not opening. He does not have a drink in front of him. He is not waiting for anyone. He glances up as you pass. He nods. Just at one of you."

### The escape

> **READ ALOUD — the exit:**
>
> "You take a staircase up. The stairs go further than they should. When you emerge it is evening, and you are miles away — on a pier over the water, shipyard cranes black against an orange sky. Behind you, where the staircase was, is a brick wall with a faded advertisement painted on it for a brand of cigarettes that hasn't been sold since 1973. It was always a brick wall."
