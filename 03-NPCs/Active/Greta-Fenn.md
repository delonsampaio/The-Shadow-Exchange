# Greta Fenn — Ministry Field Compliance Officer

**Look** — A woman in her fifties in a Ministry-gray coat, clipboard always in hand, reading glasses on a lanyard. Sensible shoes. The expression of someone who has seen everything and found all of it manageable.
**Mannerism** — Refers to everything by its form number. Never visibly surprised. Takes notes during conversations without looking at the paper.
**Drive** — The paperwork must be correct. The Ministry does not function without correct paperwork.

## Three Layers

1. **Appears as:** Ministry bureaucrat who shows up after jobs to collect incident documentation and ensure proper filing.
2. **Actually is:** Has been doing this for twenty-three years. She has seen things that would end careers. She filed the forms and moved on.
3. **Tragic Angle:** She is not numb — she is organized. The forms are how she holds it together. The day she stops filing them is the day she has to feel it.

## What She Does

- Appears during Downtime after completed scores to collect documentation
- Provides the crew with their official Ministry incident number (useful for reducing Heat complications)
- Occasionally has advance knowledge of upcoming contracts — not intentionally, but form numbers can be cross-referenced if you know what to look for
- Can expedite or slow Ministry response times through paperwork routing — she will not do this willingly, but she can be persuaded

## Comic Beats

- **Form numbers for everything.** A Barghest in a basement is "Form 47-B, Non-Standard Biological Event, Commercial Classification." A Twice-Born witness is "Form 12, Civilian Resonance Exposure — Voluntary vs. Involuntary Classification pending." She reads them aloud without irony.
- **Never surprised.** The crew produces something impossible in front of her. She says: *"That's a 91-C. I'll need two signatures."*
- **The wrong question.** She asks follow-up questions that are technically relevant but wildly mundane given the circumstances. *"And the approximate square footage of the space where the incident occurred?"*
- **The PTOLEMY situation.** She and PTOLEMY have a professional relationship. She disapproves of its filing system. It has charged her for the opinion. She paid it. She filed a form about the transaction.

## Relationship

Not an ally. Not an obstacle. A fixed point. The Ministry processes what the crew does, and she is the face of that process. She will not lie for them. She will also not go out of her way to hurt them — the paperwork is not personal.

## Voice

Ministry-adjacent but not cold. Efficient, precise, mildly put-upon in the way of someone who has been dealing with other people's chaos for two decades. When something genuinely surprises her, she pauses, makes a note, and moves on. The pause is the tell.
