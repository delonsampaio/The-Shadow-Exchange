# PTOLEMY — The Appraiser

**Look** — A piece of sentient luggage. Brass clasps, cracked leather, no visible eyes. The clasps move when it is "speaking."
**Mannerism** — Communicates through clasp patterns that translate into meaning by some process no one has ever explained. Never raises its voice — there is no voice to raise.
**Drive** — Maintain the ledger. Charge fair prices. Be useful enough that no one ever has reason to remove it.
**Secret** — *(GM-private. The bible doesn't fix this — leave it open until it matters.)*

## Three Layers

1. **Appears as** — old leather luggage, vaguely unsettling, sitting in the corner of the Subway.
2. **Actually is** — a sentient entity that appraises mystery items and Anchor Items, never lies, always charges a favor, keeps a ledger.
3. **Became this because** — *(GM-private; reserve until reveal earns its weight.)*

## Anchor Item

Almost certainly is its own anchor — the luggage *is* PTOLEMY. The implications of that have not been explored in canon.

## What it does for the crew

- **The Appraiser's Gamble** *(Custom Downtime activity)*. Take a mystery item to PTOLEMY. Roll Study or Consort.
  - **Critical:** unlock the item's hidden power and full history. PTOLEMY volunteers one additional piece of information unprompted — always relevant, never comfortable.
  - **Success:** learn how to use it safely. If Anchor Item: PTOLEMY identifies whose it is and what they look like now.
  - **Mixed:** item activates with a dangerous Glitch or Curse. If Anchor Item: its owner now knows exactly where it is.
  - **Failure:** item damaged, or Apex Aegis alerted to the crew's location. If Anchor Item: it begins broadcasting a Resonance distress signal.

## The Ledger

PTOLEMY keeps a ledger. The crew should feel vaguely indebted to PTOLEMY at all times — the ledger exists, it is accurate, and it will eventually come due.
