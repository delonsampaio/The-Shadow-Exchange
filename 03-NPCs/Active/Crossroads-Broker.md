# The Crossroads Broker

**Look** — Always in the same booth. Always alone. Dressed for a 1987 business meeting — gray suit, narrow tie, an attaché case it never opens. Never orders anything.
**Mannerism** — Listens patiently. Speaks rarely. When it does speak, it sounds like it has rehearsed the line.
**Drive** — Make deals. Take what was agreed.
**Secret** — *(GM-private. The bible leaves what it actually is open. Reserve.)*

## Three Layers

1. **Appears as** — a man waiting for an appointment.
2. **Actually is** — a deal-maker entity that grants exactly what you want and takes something you'll miss.
3. **Became this because** — unknown. Possibly always was.

## What it offers

- Clear a Wanted Clock instantly.
- Restore a Spoiled Biological to full quality.
- Restore a Siphoned Relic.
- Grant a free Critical on one roll of the crew's choice.

## What it takes

The crew agrees to "a fair price" before knowing what it is. **GM rolls d6 secretly on the Cost Table.**

| d6 | The Cost |
|---|---|
| 1 | A **Memory** — specific, chosen by the GM. Gone completely. They know they've forgotten something. They don't know what. |
| 2 | A **Relationship** — someone in the crew's life becomes distant. Won't say why. Isn't sure why. |
| 3 | A **Year** — removed from the end of their life. Non-negotiable. Non-refundable. |
| 4 | A **Future Favor** — the Broker will ask for something specific later. No details given now. Non-refusable when called. |
| 5 | A **Truth** — something they believed about themselves or their past stops being true. |
| 6 | A **Name** — someone who knew them forgets them entirely. Permanently. |

## The Rule

It never takes something the crew values lightly. It always takes something that will matter later.

## Notes

- Curious, not angry. Notes them as interesting if they fail. Offers a deal of its own if they succeed at Job 6.
- Do not introduce as a system. Introduce as a consequence.
